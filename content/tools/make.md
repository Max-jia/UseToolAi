Make (formerly Integromat) is the power user's alternative to Zapier. Where Zapier abstracts away complexity with a linear trigger-action interface, Make puts complexity front and center with a visual flow-chart-style scenario editor that shows every step, every data transformation, and every branching path. This is Make's superpower: for workflows involving 5+ steps with conditional logic, parallel execution, and data manipulation, the visual editor is dramatically more debuggable than Zapier's linear log. You can see exactly where data gets stuck, trace values through each step, and isolate issues without guesswork.

The pricing model is the other major differentiator. Zapier charges per task (every action is a task). Make charges per operation, but with a critical difference: operations are bundled and roll over. A scenario that processes 10,000 records through 5 steps uses 10,000 operations on Make (each record = 1 op) but 50,000 tasks on Zapier (each record x each step). At scale, Make is 3-5x cheaper. The free tier is also more functional: 1,000 operations/month (vs. Zapier's 100 tasks) and unlimited active scenarios with multi-step support.

Make's weaknesses are ecosystem depth and ease of use. With 2,000+ integrations, it covers most major SaaS tools but has gaps in niche apps that Zapier's 7,000+ library fills. The visual editor, while powerful, has a steeper learning curve — non-technical users often find Zapier's guided setup more approachable for simple automations. The rebrand from Integromat also means some older tutorials and community resources reference the previous name. For technical users building data pipelines and complex workflows at volume, Make is the better value. For business users who just need to connect two apps, Zapier's simplicity and integration breadth win.

Let's Enhance occupies the professional end of the AI upscaling market. Where Upscale.media handles the "I need this bigger for Instagram" use case (free, fast, web-resolution), Let's Enhance handles the "this image is going to a printing press and needs to look perfect at 300 DPI on a 24x36 inch canvas" use case. The 16x upscaling factor is the highest among consumer-priced tools, matching the capabilities of Topaz Gigapixel ($99 one-time) but with cloud processing and a subscription model.

The differentiating feature is artifact removal. JPEG compression creates blocky artifacts, old scans introduce moire patterns from the printing process, and low-light photos accumulate noise — all of which become painfully visible at large print sizes. Let's Enhance's AI doesn't just smooth them over; it reconstructs plausible detail, producing results that hold up under the magnification that print production requires. The tone and color correction is a bonus: it fixes exposure issues, white balance, and saturation as it upscales, saving a round-trip to Lightroom.

At $12/month (100 images) to $34/month (500 images), Let's Enhance is priced for professionals who bill for their work, not hobbyists. If you're upscaling 10 images a year, pay-as-you-go with Upscale.media is more economical. If you're processing hundreds of images monthly for client deliverables or an ecommerce platform, Let's Enhance's API, batch presets, and consistent output quality justify the subscription. For print professionals, it's not a luxury — it's a tool that directly generates billable deliverables from source material that would otherwise be unusable.

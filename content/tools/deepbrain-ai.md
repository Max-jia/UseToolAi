DeepBrain AI has established itself as the enterprise leader in AI avatar video production. Based in South Korea, the company's technology powers AI news anchors on Korean television, digital humans for Samsung's retail experiences, and training videos for global enterprises. The core product is deceptively simple: choose an avatar, type or paste a script (or let ChatGPT generate one), select a language, and DeepBrain produces a video of a photorealistic AI presenter delivering your content with accurate lip-sync and natural gestures.

The ChatGPT integration, added in 2025, transforms the workflow. Instead of writing scripts externally and pasting them in, you can now give DeepBrain a topic and let the AI generate the script, which the avatar then delivers. This makes it viable for rapid-turnaround content like daily news summaries, market reports, and product updates — content that would be impractical to produce with human presenters at the same frequency. The custom avatar option (Enterprise only) is what separates DeepBrain from DIY tools like Fliki: a CEO can record their own AI avatar for company-wide announcements, maintaining a personal touch without the scheduling nightmare.

DeepBrain is expensive and narrow. At $30/month for 10 minutes of video, you're paying $3/minute — comparable to low-end freelance video production. The value proposition isn't cost per minute, it's speed and scale: a training department can produce 50 multilingual training videos in a week without coordinating schedules, booking studios, or hiring voice actors for each language. For organizations already spending $500+ per training video using traditional production, DeepBrain pays for itself quickly. For individual creators or small teams, Synthesia offers a better user experience at a similar price, and HeyGen's lip-synced translation is superior for dubbing existing footage.

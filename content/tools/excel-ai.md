Rows solves the spreadsheet problem from the opposite direction of Excel. Instead of starting with formulas and hoping the data makes sense, you start with a question — "which marketing channel had the highest ROI last quarter?" — and Rows builds the analysis for you. The AI isn't bolted on as an afterthought; it's the primary interface. For business analysts who spend 80% of their time wrestling with VLOOKUP and pivot tables rather than actually thinking about the data, this alone is transformative. You can ask follow-up questions, refine the analysis conversationally, and get charts generated automatically — no chart wizard required.

The integration ecosystem is Rows' competitive moat. 100+ native connectors to tools like Slack, Salesforce, HubSpot, Google Analytics, and Stripe mean your spreadsheets pull live data instead of stale CSV exports. On the Pro plan, data refreshes every minute — making Rows a legitimate alternative to BI dashboards for teams that find Tableau or Looker overkill. The modern UI with version history and real-time collaboration makes Google Sheets feel dated by comparison. Unlimited spreadsheets on all plans (including free) is a generosity that few SaaS tools match.

Pricing is aggressive: free tier includes unlimited spreadsheets but caps AI tasks at 5/month — enough to evaluate, not to rely on. Plus at $8/user/month (200 AI tasks, daily auto-refresh) covers most small teams. Pro at $79/month base plus $8/user unlocks per-minute automation and the full integration library. The trade-offs: it's a standalone web app, not an Excel add-in, so existing spreadsheet workflows need migration. The community and template library are dwarfed by Microsoft and Google's ecosystems. But for teams building data-driven workflows from scratch — especially automated reporting and live dashboards — Rows is the spreadsheet you'd design if Excel didn't already exist.

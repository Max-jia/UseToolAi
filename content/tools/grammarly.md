Grammarly remains the most widely adopted AI writing assistant in 2026, with 40 million daily users and integrations across 500,000+ applications. Its core value proposition hasn't changed: catch errors everywhere you type, from emails to Google Docs to Slack messages, without switching tabs.

## What Grammarly Is (And Isn't)

Grammarly is an editor, not a writer. Unlike ChatGPT or Jasper, Grammarly doesn't generate content from scratch. It takes what you've already written and makes it better — fixing grammar, adjusting tone, flagging unclear sentences, and suggesting rewrites. This distinction matters: Grammarly won't make your writing sound like AI because you wrote the first draft.

## Grammarly vs AI Writing Tools

With ChatGPT and Claude now offering free, competent writing assistance, Grammarly's role has shifted. ChatGPT can write a blog post. Grammarly can't. But ChatGPT can't follow you into every app and silently catch your mistakes. Grammarly can. For professional communication — emails, reports, client messages — Grammarly's ubiquity is the differentiator.

The 2026 generative AI features (rewrite, compose, ideate) bring Grammarly closer to AI writing tools, but they're supplemental, not central. Grammarly's core remains editing and proofreading.

## Who Should Use It

Grammarly is essential for anyone who writes professionally in English. The free tier covers grammar, spelling, and basic tone detection — enough for most individual users. Premium ($12/month) adds full-sentence rewrites, plagiarism detection, and brand tone management for teams. Skip it if you only write casually or in languages other than English.

Numerous.ai brings AI to spreadsheets in the most natural way possible: with a formula. The =AI() function works exactly like =SUM() or =VLOOKUP() — type it into a cell, reference some input data, describe what you want ("classify this customer feedback as positive, negative, or neutral"), and the AI populates the cell. No API keys, no scripting, no leaving the spreadsheet. For anyone who's ever manually categorized a column of 500 customer reviews or summarized 200 support tickets one by one, the time savings are immediate and dramatic.

The =INFER() function is the standout feature. Instead of writing a prompt for every cell, you provide 3-5 examples in a column — e.g., manually classify the first five support tickets as "billing," "technical," "account," or "feature request" — and =INFER() learns the pattern and auto-classifies the remaining 500. This is faster than =AI() for bulk tasks and often more accurate because the AI is learning from your specific categorization logic rather than interpreting a prompt. For tasks like lead scoring, content tagging, and customer sentiment analysis, =INFER() replaces hours of manual work with a function call.

The limitations: Google Sheets only (no Excel support), slow processing on very large datasets, and the existential question of whether this is better than just using ChatGPT directly. For the occasional small task, copying 20 cells into ChatGPT and pasting back is free and fast. Numerous.ai becomes worth $12/month when you're processing 500+ cells regularly, when the =INFER() function saves more time than prompt engineering, or when you want non-technical teammates to access AI capabilities inside spreadsheets they already know. For Google Sheets power users processing text data at scale, it's a productivity multiplier that quickly pays for itself.

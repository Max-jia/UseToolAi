## Step 2: The Link Strategy That Doesn't Feel Like Selling

The old way: blast affiliate links everywhere and hope someone clicks. The result: zero trust, zero conversions.

**The honest way: recommend tools you use, disclose the relationship, and let the recommendation sell itself.**

### Where to place links (sorted by conversion rate):

| Placement | Why It Converts |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Tool comparison articles** | Reader is actively deciding between tools. A link at the decision point converts. |
| **"Best tools for X" roundups** | Reader is in discovery mode. Links are helpful, not pushy. |
| **How-to tutorials** | Reader is following steps. When you say "we used Semrush for keyword research," the link is contextual. |
| **Tool review pages** | Reader is evaluating a specific tool. A "Try [Tool]" CTA at the right moment = high intent. |

### The link format that users trust:

```
❌ "CLICK HERE to get the BEST AI writing tool!!!"
✅ "We use Jasper for brand-consistent content across our team.
   If you try it, here's a link (we earn a commission at no extra
   cost to you)."
```

The second version works because it's honest. The reader knows you use the tool. They know you might earn money. They respect the transparency.

## Step 3: Protect Your Commissions

Long, ugly affiliate URLs are easy to hijack. Someone replaces your ID with theirs, and you lose the commission.

**Fix: use Pretty Links (WordPress) or a custom redirect (any platform).**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| `https://xxxxx.clickbank.net/hop?sid=xxx` | `https://yoursite.com/recommends/jasper` |
| `https://semrush.sjv.io/c/xxxxx` | `https://yoursite.com/go/semrush` |

**Why this matters:**
- Users trust clean links on your domain more than third-party URLs
- Your affiliate ID is hidden from casual inspection (people can't replace it)
- Click tracking built into most redirect plugins
- FTC disclosure: still required. Add "We may earn a commission" near the link or in the footer. This is not optional.

## Step 4: Track What's Working

Don't guess which links convert. Track them.

**Free tracking setup:**
- Pretty Links (WordPress): built-in click counter per link
- Google Analytics UTM parameters: add `?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=affiliate&utm_campaign=jasper` to your redirect URLs
- Check each platform's dashboard monthly: impressions → clicks → conversions

**The one metric that matters:** revenue per article. If Article A generates $50/month from affiliate links and Article B generates $5, write more articles like Article A.

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need a blog to do affiliate marketing?

**A:** A blog is the highest-converting channel, but not the only one. YouTube descriptions, newsletter recommendations, social media — all work. The key is having an audience that trusts your recommendations, regardless of platform.

### Q: How do I disclose affiliate links without hurting conversions?

**A:** Put the disclosure where people see it, but don't make it the headline. Footer: "We may earn commissions from some links." Near each affiliate link: "(affiliate link)." Trust increases conversions more than disclosure decreases them.

### Q: Which platform should I start with?

**A:** PartnerStack. Instant approval on most programs. Jasper, Writesonic, Copy.ai, Notion — four tools you can start promoting today. Impact takes longer but unlocks Semrush ($200/sale — the highest single commission).

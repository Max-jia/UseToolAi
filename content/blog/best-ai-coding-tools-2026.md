A 103-agent deep research run tested claims about eight AI coding tools. Six produced no verifiable data. Two had numbers that survived adversarial verification.

Claude Code, Codeium, Replit AI, Lovable, Bolt.new, and v0 by Vercel all failed. Not because they are bad products. Because their publicly available data comes from marketing pages and blog aggregators, not from earnings calls or credible financial reporting.

---

## The Two With Real Numbers

### GitHub Copilot

4.7 million paid subscribers as of January 2026. Up 75% year-over-year. 90% of Fortune 100 companies are Copilot customers.

These numbers come from Microsoft's SEC-regulated earnings calls. GitHub COO Kyle Daigle disclosed the subscriber count in the FY26 Q2 call on January 28, 2026. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella confirmed the Fortune 100 penetration in July 2025. Earnings call numbers carry legal accountability.

### Cursor

$100M ARR in January 2025. $500M in June. $1B in November. $2B in February 2026. That is 20x growth in 13 months.

Bloomberg confirmed these numbers on March 2, 2026. SaaStr called it "the fastest B2B to scale ever." CNBC named Cursor to its Disruptor 50 list. Subsequent reports show Cursor surpassing $3B ARR.

---

## Why Only Two

Stack Overflow's 2025 Developer Survey confirms 84% of developers use or plan to use AI coding tools, up from 76% in 2024. This is the broadest reliable adoption data available. 49,000 respondents across 177 countries.

Everything else about the other six tools failed verification. Pricing claims came from blog aggregators with no original sourcing. User counts came from marketing materials, not financial disclosures. Benchmark scores traced back to comparison articles with no test methodology.

---

## Which One

Copilot works across VS Code, JetBrains, Neovim, Xcode, and Visual Studio. It offers Claude, GPT, and Gemini in one subscription. Enterprise compliance and admin tools are built in. Pick Copilot when you work at a company with compliance requirements or use multiple editors.

Cursor is a VS Code fork. Your Microsoft extensions will not work. But the predictive tab completion and agent mode feel woven into the editor. Developers who switch describe it as pair programming. Pick Cursor when you code four-plus hours per day and want AI that feels native.

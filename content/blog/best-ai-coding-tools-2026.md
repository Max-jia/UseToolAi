After a 103-agent deep research run on eight AI coding tools, only two had verifiable data: GitHub Copilot and Cursor. For the other six — Claude Code, Codeium/Windsurf, Replit AI, Lovable, Bolt.new, and v0 — every submitted claim failed adversarial verification. Here's what the numbers actually confirm.

---

## The Two Verified Players

### GitHub Copilot: The Enterprise Juggernaut

**4.7 million paid subscribers** as of January 2026 — up 75% year-over-year. **90% of Fortune 100 companies** are Copilot customers. These numbers come directly from Microsoft's SEC-regulated earnings calls (FY26 Q2, January 28, 2026), not from blog estimates or analyst speculation.

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella disclosed the Fortune 100 penetration in July 2025. GitHub COO Kyle Daigle confirmed the subscriber count in January 2026. When numbers come from quarterly earnings calls, they carry the weight of legal accountability for false statements.

**What this means**: Copilot isn't just the most-used AI coding tool — it's the default for enterprise. The combination of multi-model support (Claude, GPT, Gemini all available), deep GitHub integration, and Microsoft's compliance infrastructure creates a moat that competitors can't easily cross.

### Cursor: The Fastest B2B SaaS Growth Ever

Cursor's ARR trajectory, confirmed by Bloomberg (March 2, 2026):

| Date | ARR |
|------|-----|
| January 2025 | $100M |
| June 2025 | $500M |
| November 2025 | $1B |
| February 2026 | $2B |

**20x growth in 13 months.** SaaStr called it "the fastest B2B to scale ever — and it's not even close." CNBC named Cursor to its Disruptor 50 list (May 2026). Subsequent reports indicate Cursor has surpassed $3B ARR.

**What this means**: Developers aren't just trying Cursor — they're switching to it as their primary editor. The AI-native IDE experience (predictive tab completion, agentic multi-file refactoring, background agents) is compelling enough to make people leave VS Code, despite Cursor being a VS Code fork with some Microsoft extensions blocked.

## The Developer Landscape: 84% and Rising

Stack Overflow's 2025 Developer Survey (49,000+ respondents across 177 countries) confirms: **84% of developers are using or planning to use AI coding tools**, up from 76% in 2024.

This is the broadest, most methodologically sound data point available on AI coding adoption. Every other adoption statistic we tested failed verification — either the survey methodology was unspecified, the sample size was too small, or the source was a content aggregator with no original research.

## Choosing Between Copilot and Cursor

Given only two tools have verifiable data, the choice is clearer than most comparison articles suggest:

### Choose GitHub Copilot If:
- You work in an **enterprise** with compliance requirements (90% Fortune 100 penetration isn't an accident)
- You use **multiple editors** (Copilot works across VS Code, JetBrains, Neovim, Xcode, Visual Studio)
- You want the **widest model selection** (Claude, GPT, Gemini all in one subscription)
- Budget predictability matters (Copilot's pricing, while evolving, is part of Microsoft's enterprise licensing)

### Choose Cursor If:
- You're a **professional developer** who codes 4+ hours/day in a single editor
- You value **predictive tab completion** and agentic multi-file editing deeply integrated into your workflow
- You want the tool with the **fastest-growing developer community** ($2B ARR in 13 months signals product-market fit)
- You're comfortable with a VS Code fork and don't need Microsoft proprietary extensions (Pylance, Live Share)

### What About Claude Code?
Claude Code is widely discussed and clearly has momentum, but Anthropic is a private company with limited financial disclosure. The claim that Claude Code reached $2.5B ARR was specifically refuted in our research — it came from a blog aggregator (digitalapplied.com) that couldn't trace it to an original source. Claude Code may be excellent, but we can't verify its scale.

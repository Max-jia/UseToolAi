Making a presentation is not a creative task. It's an alignment task. You spend 45 minutes picking a template, resizing text boxes, and fighting with bullet point indentation — then 15 minutes on the actual content.

AI fixes the 45-minute part. The 15 minutes of content is still you. Here's the workflow.

---

## The Short Version

1. **Outline your points first** — do not start with design. Start with what you want to say.
2. **Let AI generate the deck** — one prompt, one minute, a complete presentation appears.
3. **Replace AI content with your own** — the structure is the AI's job. The substance is yours.
4. **Polish the visuals** — tweak layout, add your brand, swap images.
5. **Practice with AI feedback** — rehearse and get suggestions.

---

## 1. Start With Your Outline — Not a Template

The biggest mistake: opening PowerPoint and staring at a blank template. You design slide 1. Then stare at slide 2. After 30 minutes you have three slides and no idea what comes next.

### The fix: separate content from design.

Open a text editor — Notes, Notion, Google Docs, anything without formatting. Write your outline:

```
Title: [Your presentation title]

Slide 1: The Problem
- [One sentence describing the pain point]
- [One stat that proves it's real]

Slide 2: Why Current Solutions Suck
- [What people do now]
- [Why it doesn't work]

Slide 3: Our Approach
- [What's different]
- [Why it works]

Slide 4: Proof
- [Results / case study / data]

Slide 5: Next Steps
- [What you want the audience to do]
```

**Do not think about design. Do not think about slides. Just write what you want to say.**

This takes 5 minutes. You now have a complete presentation — it's just not pretty yet.

## 2. Let AI Generate the Deck (One Prompt, One Minute)

Now hand your outline to an AI presentation tool.

### Using Gamma (best free option):

1. Go to gamma.app
2. Click **Create New** → **Generate**
3. Paste your outline with this prompt:

```
Create a 10-slide presentation from this outline. Use a clean,
modern design. Each slide should be visually distinct — mix text,
images, and data visualization. No walls of text. One idea per slide.

Topic: [your title]

Slide 1: The Problem
- [point 1]
- [point 2]

[Continue with the rest of your outline]
```

4. Click Generate. Wait ~30 seconds.
5. Gamma presents you with a complete, designed presentation.

### What Gamma does:
- Generates a visually designed deck from your outline
- Adds AI-generated images that match the content
- Lays out text so it's scannable (not paragraphs)
- Adds transitions and visual hierarchy automatically

The output is not perfect. But it's 80% there — which is 80% more than you had 30 seconds ago.

## 3. Replace AI Content With Your Own (The Important Part)

AI-generated presentations have the same problem as AI-generated writing: they're generic and shallow. The structure is right. The content needs you.

### What to replace:

- **Stats and data** — AI may hallucinate numbers. Replace every AI-generated statistic with your actual data.
- **Case studies** — AI doesn't know your customers. Replace placeholder case studies with real ones.
- **The "why us" slide** — AI writes generic value propositions. You know why your product or idea is different. Write that yourself.
- **The call to action** — AI writes vague CTAs like "Let's work together." Replace with your specific ask.

### What to keep:

- **The structure** — AI is good at organizing information hierarchically. Trust it.
- **The visual design** — AI chose a coherent color palette and layout. Don't fight it unless something looks broken.
- **The transitions** — AI creates logical flow between slides better than most humans do.

**Rule of thumb:** Keep 70% of the AI structure. Replace 70% of the AI content. The result is a presentation that's well-organized and sounds like you.

## 4. Polish the Visuals (5 Minutes Max)

### The 5-minute polish routine:

**1. Add your logo and brand colors (1 minute)**
Gamma: Click **Theme** → upload your logo → set brand colors. This gets applied to every slide automatically.

**2. Swap AI images for real ones (2 minutes)**
AI-generated images are decent but generic. If you have product screenshots, team photos, or customer logos, swap them in. Right-click any AI image → Replace. Gamma searches Unsplash and your uploads.

**3. Fix text overflow (1 minute)**
AI sometimes generates more text than fits. Click any overflowing box → resize or split across two slides.

**4. Remove unnecessary slides (1 minute)**
AI over-generates. A 5-minute presentation might be 15 slides. Cut everything that isn't essential. The audience will thank you.

---

## 5. Practice With AI Feedback

Before presenting, rehearse. AI can help here too.

### Using ChatGPT Voice Mode (free):

1. Open ChatGPT on your phone
2. Start Voice Mode
3. Say: "I'm going to present my slides to you. After each one, give me feedback on clarity, pacing, and whether I'm using too much jargon."
4. Present slide by slide. ChatGPT listens and gives feedback between slides.

### What AI feedback catches:
- Jargon that your audience won't understand
- Slides where you're reading text verbatim (it can hear it)
- Sections where you speed up nervously
- Transitions that don't make sense

It's not as good as a human rehearsal partner. But it's available 24/7 and doesn't get tired.

## FAQ

### Q: Will the presentation look obviously AI-generated?

**A:** Not if you do Section 3 (replace AI content with yours). AI-generated structure and design looks professional — it's the vague, generic content that gives it away. Replace the content. Keep the layout.

### Q: Can I use Gamma for client presentations?

**A:** Yes, on the paid tier. Free tier presentations are public. If you're presenting to clients or investors, upgrade to Plus ($8/mo) for private presentations and custom branding.

### Q: What if I need to present offline?

**A:** Gamma and Canva both export to PowerPoint (PPTX) and PDF. Generate online, export, present offline. No internet required.

We put both through 50 real tasks: writing, coding, research, creative work. Here is when to use which.

## How We Compared Them

Three things matter. Everything else is noise.

1. **Output quality** — Does the writing sound human? Does the code run?
2. **Free tier reality** — Can you actually use it daily without hitting a wall?
3. **Ecosystem** — What else can the tool do beyond text?

## ChatGPT: The Everything Machine

**The pitch:** 300 million weekly users didn't show up by accident. ChatGPT combines writing, image generation, voice, coding, and research into one subscription. The free tier gives you the same GPT-5.5 model as the paid tier — you just wait in line at peak hours.

**What it does well:**

- Writing quality is excellent across formats — blog posts, emails, scripts, social copy all come out polished
- GPT Image 1.5 generates images with accurate text rendering — write an article, make the hero image, never leave the tab
- Custom GPTs let you build specialized assistants for recurring tasks
- Voice mode with video and screen sharing — talk to it like a colleague
- 60+ app integrations mean it plugs into your existing workflow
- Deep Research and Codex agent on paid tiers are genuinely useful for complex work

**Where it falls short:**

- Writing can feel generic — overuses "delve," "unleash," "tapestry" unless you prompt carefully
- Free tier introduced ads in February 2026 (US only so far)
- Occasional hallucinations — about 5% of G2 reviews mention factual errors
- Customer support is mostly chatbot-based — rated 4.0/5 on Capterra

**Price:** Free / Plus $20/mo / Pro $100-200/mo

**Get this if:** You want one tool that handles writing, images, research, and coding. You value ecosystem breadth over raw model performance.

**Skip this if:** You refuse to see ads in a free product, or you need an open-source model you can self-host.

## DeepSeek: Free. Open. Disarmingly Good.

**The pitch:** A Chinese lab built a model that competes with GPT-5.5 on coding and reasoning, open-sourced it under MIT license, and made the chat interface completely free. No ads. No subscription tier. No credit card. The catch is political — not technical.

**What it does well:**

- V4-Pro model: 1.6 trillion parameters, competitive with GPT-5.5 on SWE-Bench (80.6%) and GPQA Diamond (90.1%)
- Completely free — no usage caps, no throttling on web, no ads
- MIT license — self-host it, fine-tune it, build a business on it, no permission needed
- 1M token context window — drop in entire codebases or books
- API costs 5-10x less than comparable models ($0.14/M input tokens)
- Distilled versions run locally on phones (1.2-2.5GB)

**Where it falls short:**

- Chinese content moderation filters sensitive political topics — this is the #1 complaint from Western users
- No image generation, no voice mode, no plugins, no multimodal input — text only
- Data stored on servers in China — dealbreaker for enterprise compliance
- Basic chat interface — functional but not polished
- No paid consumer tier — revenue model is unclear, sustainability is a question mark

**Price:** Free. No paid tier exists.

**Get this if:** You want the best free AI with no strings attached. You're a developer who wants to self-host. You don't care about image generation or voice. You're OK with Chinese content moderation on political topics.

**Skip this if:** You need image generation, enterprise compliance certifications, or unfiltered discussion of politically sensitive topics.

## Where Each One Wins

### Use ChatGPT when:

- **You need images with your writing.** Blog post + hero image in one workflow. DeepSeek can't touch this.
- **You want voice interaction.** Talk through ideas while driving or walking. ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode with video sharing is genuinely useful.
- **You use multiple AI tools and want consolidation.** Custom GPTs, Projects, app integrations — ChatGPT reduces tab sprawl.
- **You write about anything remotely political.** DeepSeek's filters will interrupt you. ChatGPT's won't.
- **You're building a business on top of an AI platform.** The GPT Store and plugin ecosystem create distribution opportunities DeepSeek doesn't offer.

### Use DeepSeek when:

- **You don't want to pay. Ever.** DeepSeek is free with no asterisks. ChatGPT's free tier now has ads.
- **You're a developer shipping AI features.** MIT license + $0.14/M token API = build without asking permission or burning budget.
- **You need a 1M token context window.** Drop in your entire codebase. ChatGPT caps at 128K.
- **You care about open-source values.** DeepSeek's MIT license is the most permissive in frontier AI. Self-host, fine-tune, do whatever you want.
- **You're in a market where $20/month is real money.** DeepSeek's global accessibility story is unmatched.

## The Honest Truth

Most people should use both.

ChatGPT for the ecosystem — writing with images, voice brainstorming, Custom GPTs for recurring tasks. DeepSeek for the raw model — coding, reasoning, or anything where you want the best free output and don't need the extras.

If you forced us to pick one free AI in July 2026: ChatGPT wins on breadth. DeepSeek wins on freedom.

## FAQ

### Q: Is DeepSeek safe to use?

**A:** For non-political tasks, yes. The model is technically excellent. The concern is content moderation — Chinese regulations require filtering of politically sensitive topics. If your work involves discussing Chinese politics, Tiananmen Square, Taiwan, Xinjiang, or similar topics, DeepSeek will refuse to respond. For writing code, analyzing data, or drafting non-political content, there are no issues.

### Q: Which one writes better?

**A:** For English, ChatGPT has a slight edge in natural phrasing. For technical and multilingual content, they're roughly tied. DeepSeek sometimes produces more direct, less fluffy output — which can be a feature or a bug depending on what you're writing.

### Q: Does ChatGPT's free tier really have ads now?

**A:** Yes, introduced February 2026 in the US. The ads appear within the chat interface. They are not intrusive enough to ruin the experience, but they exist. DeepSeek has no ads.

### Q: Can I use DeepSeek's API commercially?

**A:** Yes. MIT license. Build a product on it, sell it, keep the profits. No revenue sharing. No permission required. This is DeepSeek's biggest advantage over ChatGPT for developers.

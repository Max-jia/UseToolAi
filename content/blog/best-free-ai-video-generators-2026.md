## How We Evaluated

Three things matter for free AI video:

1. **Output quality** — Does it look like a real video, or a melting fever dream?
2. **Free tier generosity** — How many seconds/minutes of video can you actually make?
3. **Editing capability** — Can you refine the output, or is it one-and-done?

## 1. Runway Gen-4 — The Gold Standard

**The pitch:** Runway is the Midjourney of AI video. Gen-4 produces cinematic, coherent clips that look like they came from a film set. If you want the best quality available on a free tier, this is it.

**What it does well:**
- Gen-4 model produces startlingly cinematic output — lighting, camera movement, subject consistency
- Text-to-video, image-to-video, and video-to-video in one platform
- Strong prompt adherence — "tracking shot through a rainy Tokyo alley at night" actually looks like that
- Gen-3 Alpha still available for faster, lighter generations

**Where it falls short:**
- 125 one-time credits on free tier — use them and they're gone, no daily refresh
- Generations max out at ~10 seconds
- Advanced features (motion brush, camera controls) are paywalled

**Price:** Free (125 one-time credits) / Standard $15/mo / Unlimited $95/mo

**Get this if:** You want the best-looking AI video possible without paying. Spend your 125 credits carefully — each one matters.

**Skip this if:** You need ongoing free access. Runway's free tier is a tasting menu, not a meal plan.

## 2. Pika — The Image-to-Video Specialist

**The pitch:** Pika excels at one thing: taking a still image and bringing it to life. Upload a photo, describe the motion you want, and Pika animates it. Perfect for social media content.

**What it does well:**
- Best-in-class image-to-video — subtle movements, natural physics
- Lip-sync feature: upload an image of a face + audio, and it animates the mouth
- 30 credits/day on free tier — daily refresh means you can experiment regularly
- Simple interface — upload, describe, generate

**Where it falls short:**
- Text-to-video quality lags behind Runway
- Clips are short (3-5 seconds typically)
- Limited editing — mostly generate-and-download

**Price:** Free (30 credits/day) / Starter $8/mo / Pro $30/mo

**Get this if:** You have photos you want to animate — product shots, portraits, landscapes. Pika's image-to-video is the best free option.

**Skip this if:** You need text-to-video generation. Runway or Kling are stronger.

## 3. HeyGen — AI Avatars That Don't Look Creepy

**The pitch:** HeyGen creates videos of AI avatars speaking your script. Upload text, pick an avatar (or upload your own face), and get a talking-head video. Perfect for training videos, product demos, and personalized outreach.

**What it does well:**
- Avatars look surprisingly natural — lip sync, facial expressions, head movement
- Upload your own face to create a personal AI avatar
- 175+ languages with natural-sounding voices
- Templates for common use cases (sales outreach, training, announcements)

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier: 1 minute of video per month — extremely limited
- Custom avatar creation requires paid plan
- Uncanny valley is still present — convincing but not perfect

**Price:** Free (1 min/month) / Creator $24/mo / Business $149/mo

**Get this if:** You need to create talking-head videos without being on camera yourself.

**Skip this if:** You need more than 1 minute per month. The free tier is a demo.

## 4. Descript — Edit Video Like a Document

**The pitch:** Descript's killer feature: it transcribes your video into text, and you edit the video by editing the text. Delete a sentence in the transcript, and that section disappears from the video. It feels like magic the first time.

**What it does well:**
- Text-based video editing — delete words to cut footage, paste text to add AI-generated voiceover
- AI-powered filler word removal — "um," "uh," "like" disappear with one click
- Screen recording + webcam in one tool — perfect for tutorials and demos
- Studio Sound: one-click audio cleanup that actually works

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier: 1 export per month — you can edit all you want, but only get one finished file
- AI voice cloning requires paid plan
- Watermark on free exports

**Price:** Free (1 export/month, watermarked) / Hobbyist $16/mo / Creator $24/mo

**Get this if:** You edit talking-head videos, tutorials, or podcasts. The text-based editing workflow is a genuine productivity breakthrough.

**Skip this if:** You need multiple exports per month. The free tier is for trying the workflow.

## 5. Luma Dream Machine — B-Roll and Atmospheric Clips

**The pitch:** Luma generates dreamy, atmospheric video clips that work beautifully as B-roll, backgrounds, and visual texture. It's not for precise, controlled output — it's for mood.

**What it does well:**
- Strong at cinematic, atmospheric shots — landscapes, cityscapes, abstract visuals
- Photorealistic output that doesn't scream "AI generated"
- Looping capability for seamless backgrounds
- 30 generations/month on free tier

**Where it falls short:**
- Less control than Runway — prompt adherence is looser
- Human subjects can look distorted in motion
- Clips max out at ~5 seconds

**Price:** Free (30 gens/month) / Standard $9.99/mo / Pro $49.99/mo

**Get this if:** You need B-roll, backgrounds, or atmospheric clips for video projects. Luma's visual style is distinct and useful.

**Skip this if:** You need precise control over output or human subjects that look realistic in motion.

## 6. InVideo — Social Media Videos, No Editing Skills Required

**The pitch:** InVideo is built for one thing: turning a text prompt into a ready-to-post social media video. It generates the script, finds stock footage, adds captions, and picks music — all automatically.

**What it does well:**
- Full pipeline: script → footage → captions → music → export
- Templates for YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook
- AI voiceover in multiple languages
- 10 minutes of export per week on free tier

**Where it falls short:**
- Stock footage can feel generic — the AI picks from a library, it doesn't generate original video
- Free exports include InVideo branding
- Less creative control than manual editing

**Price:** Free (10 mins/week, branded) / Plus $17/mo / Max $48/mo

**Get this if:** You need to produce social media videos regularly and don't want to learn video editing.

**Skip this if:** You want original AI-generated footage rather than stock clips.

## 7. Kling — China's Answer to Sora

**The pitch:** Kling is the most technically impressive AI video generator most people haven't heard of. Developed by Kuaishou (China's second-largest short-video platform), it generates remarkably coherent 2-minute clips — longer than most competitors.

**What it does well:**
- Up to 2 minutes of continuous generation — Runway and Pika max out at 10 seconds
- Strong physics simulation — objects fall, water flows, people walk naturally
- 1080p output on free tier
- Daily free credits refresh

**Where it falls short:**
- Interface is Chinese-first — English support exists but feels secondary
- Content moderation follows Chinese regulations
- Slower generation times than Western competitors

**Price:** Free (daily credits) / Standard $10/mo / Pro $37/mo

**Get this if:** You need longer video clips (30+ seconds) and are comfortable navigating a Chinese-first interface.

**Skip this if:** You want a polished English-language experience or your content involves politically sensitive topics.

**The pitch:** Seedance specializes in stylized, artistic video generation — particularly anime and illustration-style animation. If you want your images to move with a hand-drawn aesthetic, this is the tool.

**What it does well:**
- Exceptional anime-style video generation — character expressions, fight scenes, ambient animation
- Strong at artistic and illustrated styles, not just photorealism
- Developed by Jimeng (ByteDance), backed by serious R&D
- Daily free credits

**Where it falls short:**
- Photorealism is not the focus — don't expect Runway-level realism
- Chinese-first interface, English support is minimal
- Niche use case — great for anime creators, less useful for general video needs

**Price:** Free (daily credits) / API from ¥0.023/1K tokens

**Get this if:** You create anime, manga, or illustrated content and want to animate it. Seedance is purpose-built for you.

**Skip this if:** You need photorealism or have no interest in anime-style output.

## 9. CapCut — The Free Editor That Happens to Have AI

**The pitch:** CapCut (by ByteDance, the TikTok company) is a full-featured video editor that's completely free — no watermark, no time limit. It now includes AI features: auto-captions, background removal, and AI-generated effects.

**What it does well:**
- Completely free — no watermark, no export limits, no time caps
- Auto-captions in 20+ languages with excellent accuracy
- AI background removal without a green screen
- AI motion tracking, color grading, and effects
- Built-in templates optimized for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts

**Where it falls short:**
- AI text-to-video generation is limited compared to Runway or Pika
- Heavily optimized for short-form vertical video — less suited for horizontal/landscape
- ByteDance-owned — data privacy concerns for some users

**Price:** Free / Pro $7.99/mo

**Get this if:** You edit short-form videos for social media and want AI features without paying. CapCut's free tier is the most generous on this list.

**Skip this if:** You need AI text-to-video generation. CapCut is an editor with AI features, not an AI video generator.

## FAQ

### Q: Why are free AI video tiers so much worse than free AI image tiers?

**A:** Video generation requires 100-1,000x more computing power than image generation. A single 5-second video at 24fps is 120 frames. Each frame is roughly equivalent to generating one AI image. Free tiers exist, but they're inherently more constrained than image generators.

### Q: Do any of these generate usable audio with the video?

**A:** Most generate silent video. InVideo and Canva can add AI voiceover and music. HeyGen's avatars speak with AI voices. For anything beyond basic voiceover, you'll pair these with a separate AI audio tool.

### Q: Which one is best for TikTok and Reels?

**A:** CapCut. It's made by the TikTok company, optimized for short-form vertical video, and completely free. Use Pika or Runway to generate the clips, then CapCut to edit, caption, and export.

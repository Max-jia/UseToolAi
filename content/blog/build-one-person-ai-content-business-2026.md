## The Workflow

### Step 1: Find What People Are Actually Searching For

You don't guess what to write about. You find out.

**Tool: Perplexity (free, 5 Pro searches/day)**

Open Perplexity. Use your 5 daily Pro searches strategically. Ask:

```
What are the most common questions people ask about [YOUR TOPIC]?
Include search volume estimates and identify content gaps in existing articles.
```

Perplexity searches the web, reads existing articles, and tells you what's missing. This replaces hours of manual SERP analysis.

Then switch to free tools for validation:

- **Google Search Console** (free) — if you already have a site, look at what queries bring impressions but no clicks. These are content gaps you own.
- **AnswerThePublic** (free tier) — enter a topic, get a visual map of questions people ask. Export the CSV. That's your editorial calendar.
- **Google "People Also Ask"** — search your topic. Expand every PAA box. Those are article subtopics.

**The output:** A list of 20-50 article topics that real people are searching for. Not guesses. Data.

---

### Step 2: Research Without Reading 50 Tabs

You don't need to read every article on page one of Google. Let AI do the synthesis.

**Tool: Perplexity (standard search — unlimited free)**

For each article topic:

```
Summarize the top 5 ranking articles for "[YOUR TOPIC]".
What points do they all cover? What's missing from all of them?
Give me the 5 key facts, the 3 most cited statistics, and the 2 biggest gaps.
```

Perplexity's standard search is unlimited and cited. Every claim links to its source. You now have a research brief in 60 seconds.

---

### Step 3: Write the First Draft

**Tool: Claude (free, ~20-30 messages/day)**

This is where the workflow diverges from "ask AI to write an article."

**The prompt that works:**

```
You are writing for [AUDIENCE]. Topic: [TOPIC].

Structure:
- Open with a specific, surprising fact or number (1-2 sentences)
- Section 1: [POINT 1] — include one specific example
- Section 2: [POINT 2] — include one data point
- Section 3: [POINT 3] — include one practical takeaway
- Close with "what to do next"

Rules:
- NEVER use: delve, unleash, tapestry, landscape, crucial, furthermore, game-changer, revolutionary
- Max 3 sentences per paragraph
- Write at an 8th-grade reading level
- Include one "here's what I actually did" sentence per section
- Do not write a generic introduction or conclusion

Here is my research:

[Paste Perplexity output from Step 2]
```

I use Claude instead of ChatGPT for first drafts because Claude's prose sounds less like a robot. ChatGPT needs more editing to kill the AI voice. When my name is on the article, I want the first draft as close to human as possible.

One article takes one Claude message. With 20-30 free messages a day, that's 5-7 drafts. More than enough.

---

### Step 4: Make It Sound Like You

AI output is a first draft. The editing pass is where the writing actually happens.

**Tool: Grammarly (free)**

Run the draft through Grammarly. Accept the grammar fixes. Ignore most of the style suggestions — they make everything sound corporate.

**The human editing checklist (15 minutes per article):**

- [ ] Delete the first paragraph. AI intros are always generic. Your real opening is paragraph two.
- [ ] Split any sentence over 25 words into two sentences.
- [ ] Add one personal anecdote per section. AI can't do this. "When I first tried this..." or "A mistake I made..."
- [ ] Add one strong opinion. AI is balanced to a fault. "Most SEO advice is garbage." The AI won't say this. You should.
- [ ] Delete hedging words: "generally," "typically," "it could be argued." State things plainly.
- [ ] Vary rhythm. Short. Punchy. Then a slightly longer sentence that flows. Then short again.

**Time budget for a 1,500-word article:**
- Research (Perplexity): 5 minutes
- First draft (Claude): 2 minutes
- Human edit: 15 minutes
- Images (ChatGPT): 3 minutes
- SEO polish + publish: 5 minutes

**Total: 30 minutes per article.**

---

### Step 5: Create Images That Don't Scream "AI Made This"

**Tool: ChatGPT (GPT Image 1.5, free)**

For each article, generate 1-2 images. The prompt matters.

**Bad:** "a picture of a person working on a laptop"
**Good:** "editorial photo of a minimalist desk setup at golden hour, MacBook with writing visible on screen, coffee cup, natural light from window, 85mm lens, shallow depth of field, slight film grain"

Add imperfections to the prompt: `film grain`, `natural skin texture`, `slight underexposure`. These tell the AI to break the smooth, plastic look.

For charts and diagrams: screenshots of actual data from your Google Search Console or Perplexity research. Real data beats AI-generated illustrations every time.

---

### Step 6: Ship It

The final step is the one most people skip. You have a drafted, edited, illustrated article. Publish it.

If you're starting from zero:
- **Newsletter:** Buttondown (free for up to 100 subscribers)
- **Blog:** Vercel + Next.js (free hobby tier)
- **Social distribution:** Twitter/X thread summarizing the article, LinkedIn post, relevant Reddit communities

**The publishing rhythm that works:**
- 2-3 articles per week
- Each article becomes: 1 Twitter thread + 1 LinkedIn post + 1 Reddit post (if the community allows it)
- One article becomes a "lead magnet" for newsletter signups

## The Monetization Path

You're building an audience. Here is how it pays:

### Month 1-3: Affiliate Revenue

Link to the tools you recommend. Every article that mentions a tool includes an affiliate link. The tools in this stack (Perplexity, Claude, ChatGPT, Grammarly) all have affiliate or referral programs. You're recommending tools you actually use. The affiliate revenue is a byproduct, not the goal.

**Realistic month 3:** $50-200/month from affiliate links embedded in existing articles.

### Month 3-6: Newsletter Sponsorships

When your newsletter hits 1,000 subscribers, sponsorships become viable. AI tool companies pay $100-500 per issue to reach an audience actively looking for tools.

**Realistic month 6:** $200-500/month from one sponsored issue per month.

### Month 6-12: Digital Products

Package your expertise. Create a "Content Business Starter Kit" — templates, prompt library, workflow checklist. Sell it for $29. Your audience already trusts your recommendations because you've been giving them away for free for 6 months.

**Realistic month 12:** $500-2,000/month combined (affiliate + sponsorships + digital products).

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need to be a good writer?

**A:** No. You need to be a good editor. AI writes the first draft. You make it sound like a human. The editing skills come with practice. After 20 articles, you'll be able to edit an AI draft in 10 minutes.

### Q: Won't Google penalize AI-assisted content?

**A:** No — if it's good. Google's official position: AI-generated content is fine as long as it's helpful to users. Heavily edited AI content with personal anecdotes and original data is indistinguishable from fully human-written content.

### Q: How do I choose a niche?

**A:** Pick the intersection of: (1) something you know enough about to write 50 articles, (2) something people search for, (3) something with affiliate products. AI tools are one niche. Personal finance, fitness, parenting, cooking, travel — the workflow works for any information-rich topic.

## How We Evaluated

Three things actually matter for image generation:

1. **Output quality** — Does it look like a photo or a painting, or does it look like AI slop? We tested the same prompt across all tools to compare.
2. **Free tier reality** — How many images can you actually make per day before the faucet turns off?
3. **Feature set** — Can it do inpainting, outpainting, style transfer, or is it a one-button generator?

## 1. ChatGPT (GPT Image 1.5) — The All-Rounder That Accidentally Won Images

**The pitch:** ChatGPT wasn't built for image generation. But GPT Image 1.5 changed the game — it renders text accurately, follows complex prompts, and is built into the tool 300M people already use.

**What it does well:**
- Text rendering is now genuinely good — logos, posters, and memes come out readable
- Understands complex prompts — "a cozy reading nook in a Victorian library, golden hour light, photorealistic, 85mm lens" actually works
- Built into ChatGPT free tier — no separate account, no separate app
- You can iterate conversationally — "make the cat orange" instead of re-typing the entire prompt

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier queues at peak hours — same GPT-5.5 model, just slower
- Style range is narrower than Midjourney — it skews toward photorealism and clean illustration
- No fine-tuning or model selection — you get one default style

**Price:** Free (with ChatGPT free tier) / Plus $20/mo

**Get this if:** You already use ChatGPT and want images without opening another tab. You need text in your images that's actually readable.

**Skip this if:** You need Midjourney-level artistic range or want to train custom styles.

## 2. Leonardo AI — The Best Free Tier in the Game

**The pitch:** Leonardo gives you 150 free credits every single day. That's roughly 30-50 images. Every day. For free. No other tool on this list matches that volume on the free tier.

**What it does well:**
- Daily credit refresh — 150 credits resets every 24 hours, not monthly
- Character consistency — upload a reference image and generate variations that keep the same face/outfit
- Specialized models: anime, cinematic, fantasy art, product photography — pick your style
- Real-time canvas for inpainting and iterative editing
- Community feed for prompt inspiration (and copying prompts that work)

**Where it falls short:**
- No text-in-image capability — don't try to generate logos or posters here
- Free tier images are public by default (paid tiers get private generation)
- Some advanced models are paywalled

**Price:** Free (150 credits/day) / Apprentice $12/mo / Artisan $30/mo

**Get this if:** You're a game developer, D&D dungeon master, or anyone who needs consistent characters across multiple images. Or you just want the most generous free tier available.

**Skip this if:** You need text in your images or private generation.

## 3. Ideogram — When Text in Images Actually Matters

**The pitch:** Every AI image generator claims it can do text. Most can't. Ideogram can. If you're making logos, posters, greeting cards, or memes, this is the one.

**What it does well:**
- Best-in-class text rendering — 78% first-generation accuracy (compared to ~55% for competitors)
- Clean, design-oriented aesthetic — leans graphic design rather than photorealism
- Magic Prompt feature auto-improves your prompts
- Simple interface — type text, pick a style, get results

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier: 10 slow generations per day — enough for casual use, not for volume
- Style range is narrower — design-forward, less suited for photorealistic portraits
- Slower generation on free tier — "slow" is in the name

**Price:** Free (10 slow gens/day) / Basic $8/mo / Plus $20/mo

**Get this if:** You make social media graphics, event posters, or anything with words on images. Ideogram's text accuracy beats everyone else.

**Skip this if:** You need photorealism or high-volume free generation.

## 4. Canva AI — Design Templates Meet AI Images

**The pitch:** Canva's AI image generator is not the best image generator. But it lives inside Canva — the design tool 200M people already use. If you're making social posts, presentations, or marketing materials, the integration is the point.

**What it does well:**
- AI image generation inside Canva's editor — generate, resize, add text, and export in one flow
- Magic Media generates images, graphics, and short videos
- Templates + AI images = finished designs in minutes, not hours
- Brand kit integration keeps colors and fonts consistent

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier: 50 lifetime credits total — not 50/month, 50 ever. Use them wisely.
- Image quality is good-not-great — fine for social media, insufficient for print
- AI features are scattered across different tools (Magic Media, Magic Eraser, etc.)

**Price:** Free (50 lifetime AI credits) / Pro $15/mo / Team $30/mo

**Get this if:** You're already a Canva user making social media content. The AI image generator is a bonus, not the main event.

**Skip this if:** You need high-volume image generation. 50 lifetime credits is a tasting menu, not a meal.

## 5. Google Nano Banana (Gemini) — Creepily Good Realism

**The pitch:** Google's latest image model, accessible for free in the Gemini app, produces some of the most realistic AI photos available. If you want images that don't trigger the "this is AI" instinct, try this.

**What it does well:**
- Exceptional photorealism — lighting, skin texture, and shadows look natural
- Fast generation — most images render in under 5 seconds
- Built into Google Gemini — free tier, no separate download
- Handles complex scenes without melting into surrealism

**Where it falls short:**
- Google's content filters are aggressive — some prompts get blocked for unclear reasons
- Less stylistic range — photorealism is the strength, illustration is not
- No advanced editing (inpainting, outpainting)

**Price:** Free (via Gemini app) / Gemini Pro $19.99/mo

**Get this if:** You need realistic photos that don't look AI-generated. Product mockups, lifestyle photos, anything where the uncanny valley is your enemy.

**Skip this if:** You want artistic, illustrated, or stylized output. Nano Banana's realism is its superpower and its limitation.

## 6. Bing Image Creator (DALL-E 4) — The Easiest Way to Access DALL-E

**The pitch:** Microsoft gives you free access to OpenAI's DALL-E 4 through Bing. No ChatGPT account needed. Just go to bing.com/create and start typing.

**What it does well:**
- Powered by DALL-E 4 — OpenAI's dedicated image model, not the one built into ChatGPT
- 15 fast generations per day — "fast" means under 10 seconds
- After you use your 15 fast ones, you still get unlimited slower generations
- Simple interface — type a prompt, get four variations, pick the best

**Where it falls short:**
- Microsoft's content moderation is stricter than OpenAI's — more prompts get rejected
- No editing tools — generate only, no inpainting or variations
- Boosts expire daily, they don't roll over

**Price:** Free (15 fast gens/day, unlimited slow)

**Get this if:** You want DALL-E quality without a ChatGPT account. The unlimited slow tier means you can experiment endlessly.

**Skip this if:** You need to edit images after generating them. Bing is generate-only.

## 7. Adobe Firefly — The Only Option If Copyright Matters

**The pitch:** Firefly is the only AI image generator trained exclusively on licensed content (Adobe Stock). If you're making images for commercial use and want legal safety, this is the only game in town.

**What it does well:**
- Trained on Adobe Stock — no copyright grey area, Adobe offers IP indemnification
- Generative Fill in Photoshop is genuinely magical for retouching
- Text-to-image, text effects, and recoloring in one tool
- Integrates with Adobe Express (free) and Creative Cloud (paid)

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier: 25 credits/month — the stingiest on this list
- Photorealism lags behind competitors (rated 7-8/10 vs Midjourney's 9/10)
- Text rendering is poor — Adobe's own documentation acknowledges this limitation
- The realism gap is a deliberate trade-off for legal safety

**Price:** Free (25 credits/month) / Premium $4.99/mo

**Get this if:** You use AI images commercially and need to prove your images are legally defensible. Or if you already pay for Creative Cloud and get Firefly included.

**Skip this if:** You need high-volume free generation or photorealistic output. Firefly is a legal tool as much as a creative one.

## 8. Stable Diffusion (Open-Source) — Total Control, Zero Cost

**The pitch:** Stable Diffusion is not a website. It's an open-source model you can run on your own computer for free, forever, with no content filters, no credit systems, and no one telling you what you can and can't generate.

**What it does well:**
- Completely free — no credits, no limits, no accounts
- Run it locally — your GPU, your rules, your privacy
- Massive community: thousands of custom models (CivitAI alone hosts 50,000+)
- Full control: inpainting, outpainting, img2img, ControlNet for pose/composition guidance
- No content moderation — generate whatever you want

**Where it falls short:**
- Requires a decent GPU (6GB+ VRAM recommended) — not accessible on phones or cheap laptops
- Setup is not beginner-friendly — you'll follow a tutorial, not click a button
- Quality depends on the model you pick and the prompt you write — less hand-holding than commercial tools

**Price:** Free (open-source) / DreamStudio credits from $10

**Get this if:** You're technically comfortable, want total creative control, or need to generate images without content restrictions.

**Skip this if:** You want a one-click web experience. Stable Diffusion rewards tinkerers, not convenience-seekers.

## 10. Midjourney Is Not on This List. Here's Why.

The elephant in the room.

Midjourney V7 produces the best AI images overall — higher photorealism scores (9/10), better artistic range, and a massive community sharing prompts and techniques. It is the gold standard.

It also has no free tier. Zero.

You can't even sign up without a subscription. The cheapest plan is $10/month for roughly 200 images. If you're serious about AI image generation, Midjourney is worth the money. If you're looking for free, the tools above are your best alternatives.

**The best free Midjourney alternative is Leonardo AI.** It gives you 150 free credits per day with good quality and model variety. It won't match Midjourney's photorealism ceiling, but it's the closest thing at zero cost.

## Which One Should You Pick?

**You want one tool that does everything well**
→ **ChatGPT (GPT Image)**. You probably already use ChatGPT for text. The image generation is built in and surprisingly good. Text rendering, photorealism, illustration — it handles all of them competently.

**You need high volume for free**
→ **Leonardo AI**. 150 credits every 24 hours. No other free tier comes close to this volume. The character consistency features are a bonus.

**You make logos, posters, or social graphics with text**
→ **Ideogram**. Nobody beats it on text rendering. The 10 free generations per day are slow but the quality justifies the wait.

**You're worried about copyright and commercial use**
→ **Adobe Firefly**. It's the only model trained exclusively on licensed content with IP indemnification. The free tier is small (25 credits/month) but the legal peace of mind is real.

**You're technical and want zero limits**
→ **Stable Diffusion**. Run it locally. No credits, no filters, no one watching. The trade-off is setup complexity.

**You want Midjourney quality without paying**
→ You can't. That's the honest answer. **Leonardo AI** is the closest free alternative. But if image generation is core to your work, Midjourney's $10/month is the best $10 you'll spend on AI tools.

## FAQ

### Q: Do AI-generated images belong to me?

**A:** It depends on the tool and where you live. In the US, purely AI-generated images currently cannot be copyrighted (US Copyright Office guidance). But most commercial tools grant you full usage rights for the images you create. Adobe Firefly is the safest — trained on licensed content with contractual IP indemnification. Stable Diffusion's legal status is murkier since it was trained on publicly scraped images.

### Q: Which free tool produces the most realistic images?

**A:** Google Nano Banana and ChatGPT (GPT Image 1.5) are tied for photorealism on free tiers. Nano Banana sometimes edges ahead on skin texture and lighting, but ChatGPT wins on prompt understanding.

### Q: Why do AI images still have messed-up hands?

**A:** They mostly don't anymore. GPT Image 1.5, Midjourney V7, and Nano Banana all handle hands correctly in the majority of generations. If you're still seeing six-fingered mutants, you're probably using an older model or a less capable free tool.

### Q: Can I use these for print (posters, merchandise)?

**A:** Most free tiers output at 1024x1024 or similar — fine for social media and web, insufficient for print. For print-resolution images, you'll need a paid tier (Midjourney, Leonardo paid, or upscaling with a separate tool).

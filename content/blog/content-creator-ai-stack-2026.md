## 1. Research: Perplexity

Stop guessing what to make. Find out what people are already searching for.

**The prompt:**
```
What are the most common questions people ask about [TOPIC]?
What existing videos on this topic are missing?
Give me 5 video ideas with specific angles.
```

Perplexity cites its sources. You can click through to verify. This replaces two hours of manually browsing YouTube search results and reading Reddit threads.

**Free tier:** 5 Pro searches/day. Use them for topic research. Regular unlimited search for follow-up questions.

---

## 2. Scripting: Claude

ChatGPT can write a script. Claude writes one that sounds like you talking, not a robot reading a Wikipedia article.

**The prompt:**
```
Write a YouTube script about [TOPIC].
Tone: conversational, like you're explaining to a friend.
Structure:
- Hook (5 seconds): surprising fact or question
- Body: 3 main points with examples
- Outro: one clear takeaway + call to action

Rules: under 1,500 words. No jargon. No "in today's video."
Read at an 8th grade level.
```

**Why Claude over ChatGPT:** Claude's prose sounds more human. For a script you'll be reading out loud, natural phrasing matters more than perfect grammar.

**Free tier:** ~20-30 messages/day. Enough for 5-7 script drafts daily.

---

## 3. Thumbnails: ChatGPT (GPT Image)

Thumbnails get the click. GPT Image generates them from a prompt.

**The prompt:**
```
YouTube thumbnail for a video titled "[TITLE]".
Bold, high contrast, warm colors. Face with surprised
expression (not real person, illustrated). Clean
composition. 16:9 aspect ratio. No text on the image.
```

If you need text on the thumbnail, switch to Ideogram (free tier) for better text rendering. ChatGPT is better at the images. Ideogram is better at putting words on them.

---

## 4. B-roll: Pexels + Pixabay

AI-generated video is not ready for prime time yet. Stock footage is.

**Workflow:**
1. Read your script. Highlight every noun and action verb.
2. Search each on Pexels and Pixabay
3. Download 2-3 clips per keyword
4. You'll use maybe half. That's fine. Options > no options.

Both sites are free. No attribution required. Search by keyword, download the highest resolution available.

---

## 5. Music: Suno

Background music sets the tone. Suno generates original tracks so you never worry about copyright strikes.

**The prompt:**
```
Upbeat lo-fi instrumental, 90 BPM, background music for a
tech explainer video. No vocals. Warm, positive energy.
```

Download the track. Import into CapCut. Set volume to 15-20% so it doesn't compete with your voice.

**Free tier:** 5 generations/day. Save the good ones. After a week you'll have a library of 10-15 original tracks you can reuse.

---

## 6. Voiceover: ElevenLabs

Don't want to record your own voice? ElevenLabs generates natural-sounding narration.

**The workflow:**
1. Paste your Claude-written script
2. Pick a voice (dozens available, sorted by style)
3. Adjust speed to 0.9x (slightly slower sounds more natural)
4. Generate
5. Download as MP3

**Free tier:** 10 minutes of generated audio per month. One 8-10 minute video uses most of it. For multiple videos per month, upgrade to Starter ($5/month).

---

## 7. Editing: CapCut

CapCut is free. No watermark. Made by the TikTok company. Optimized for vertical video, works for horizontal too.

**The 10-minute edit workflow:**
1. Import audio (your ElevenLabs file or your own recording)
2. Drag B-roll onto the timeline
3. Align clip changes with sentence breaks in the audio
4. Add background music at 15-20% volume
5. Add auto-captions
6. Export

No color grading. No fancy transitions. No effects. Good content does not need them.

---

## 8. Captions: CapCut Auto-Captions

CapCut transcribes your audio with ~95% accuracy. The remaining 5% are proper nouns and technical terms.

**The 2-minute caption fix:**
1. Click Text → Auto Captions
2. Let it process
3. Scroll through. Fix the 5% that are wrong.
4. Choose a caption style (clean sans-serif, no animations)

Captioned videos get higher watch time. Many people watch with sound off.

---

## 9. YouTube SEO: TubeBuddy

TubeBuddy is a browser extension that tells you how to title and tag your videos.

**Free tier features:**
- Tag suggestions based on what top-ranking videos use
- Search volume estimates for keywords
- Competitor tag analysis

The free tier is limited but useful. For serious YouTube SEO, VidIQ's free tier is the alternative.

---

## 10. Publishing: Buffer

Record once. Publish everywhere. Buffer schedules posts across platforms.

**Free tier:** 3 social channels. Connect YouTube, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn. Write one post, schedule it everywhere.

**The 30-second cross-post:**
1. Upload video to YouTube
2. Copy the description
3. Paste into Buffer → post to Twitter + LinkedIn
4. Schedule for optimal posting time

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need all 10 tools?

**A:** No. Start with Claude (scripting), CapCut (editing + captions), and Pexels (B-roll). Those three cover 80% of the time savings. Add the others as you need them.

### Q: Will my audience know I used AI?

**A:** Not if you edit. AI scripts need a human pass. AI thumbnails need a human check. AI captions need human corrections. The tools accelerate. The human makes it good.

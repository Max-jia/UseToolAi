## Step 1: Don't Generate Random Images. Find What's Already Selling.

The #1 mistake: generating images you like, then hoping someone buys them.

**What to do instead:**

1. Go to `contributor.stock.adobe.com` — create a free contributor account
2. Click **Insights** in the dashboard
3. Look at the trending search terms and "content gaps." These are photos people are searching for but can't find.

**Consistent high-demand categories:**
- Office / remote work scenes
- Nature and travel landscapes
- Food photography
- Technology / AI concept images
- Lifestyle / fitness

Pick a niche. Generate 50-100 images in that niche. Don't scatter across categories. The algorithm rewards depth.

---

## Step 2: The Prompt That Produces Stock-Ready Images

Generic Midjourney prompts produce AI-looking slop. Stock reviewers reject those.

**The stock-ready prompt template:**

```
A professional stock photo of [subject], [setting],
clean composition, natural lighting, shallow depth of field,
photorealistic, commercial use --ar 16:9 --style raw
--no watermark, text, signature, logo
```

**What each parameter does:**

| Parameter | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| `--ar 16:9` | Landscape ratio — most stock buyers need this |
| `--style raw` | Reduces Midjourney's default "artistic" look — more photographic |
| `--no watermark, text, signature` | Watermarks = automatic rejection |

**Real examples that passed Adobe Stock review:**

```
A professional stock photo of a diverse team in a modern office
with plants, natural window lighting, collaborative atmosphere,
photorealistic, clean composition --ar 16:9 --style raw
--no watermark, text, signature
```

```
A professional stock photo of a healthy breakfast bowl on a
marble counter, morning sunlight, shallow depth of field,
photorealistic, food photography --ar 4:3 --style raw
--no watermark, text, signature
```

## Step 3: Post-Processing — The Step Most People Skip

Adobe Stock rejects AI images for one reason more than any other: **resolution too low.**

Midjourney outputs at 2048×2048. Stock platforms want 4000+ pixels on the long edge.

| Task | Tool | Cost |
|------|------|------|
| Upscale to 4000px+ | **Topaz Gigapixel** (best quality) | $99 one-time |
| Upscale (free alternative) | **Upscale.media** | Free |
| Remove background | **Adobe Express** | Free |
| Spot-check for AI artifacts | Your eyes | Free |

**The spot-check checklist before uploading:**
- [ ] Hands — correct number of fingers?
- [ ] Teeth — not a continuous white blob?
- [ ] Text in image — readable and correct? (If not, crop it out)
- [ ] Eyes — pupils match, not alien-looking?
- [ ] Edges — no weird blurring where objects meet?

One bad image gets your entire portfolio flagged. Check every image.

---

## Step 4: Upload Like a Professional

1. Adobe Stock Contributor → **Upload**
2. Title: describe exactly what's in the image in English. "Diverse team collaborating in modern office with natural light" not "office photo"
3. Keywords: 25-50 precise tags. Include: subject, setting, lighting style, composition, emotion, season, color palette
4. Check **"This content was created using generative AI"** — required. Skipping this gets you banned
5. Submit for review. Typical wait: 3-7 days

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need Midjourney? Can I use free tools?

**A:** Midjourney produces the most consistently stock-ready images. ChatGPT (GPT Image) and Leonardo AI are viable free alternatives. The quality floor matters for stock — one rejected batch means wasted upload time. Midjourney's $10/month is the best investment in this workflow.

### Q: Will stock platforms ban AI images in the future?

**A:** The opposite. Adobe, Shutterstock, and Getty are all actively building AI image tools and accepting AI submissions. Their AI content policies are expanding, not contracting. The window is open.

### Q: How long until I make real money?

**A:** 3-6 months. Stock is a volume game. Month 1: upload 100 images. Month 2: upload 100 more. By month 4, your portfolio has aged and the algorithm has learned which images convert. The income curve is flat for months 1-3 then begins to rise.

## How We Evaluated

## 1. ChatGPT — The Productivity Swiss Army Knife

**The pitch:** ChatGPT is not a productivity tool. It's every productivity tool. Research, writing, brainstorming, data analysis, coding — it does all of them well enough that you'll reach for it before any specialized tool.

**What it does well:**
- GPT-5.5 handles complex multi-step tasks — "read this PDF, summarize the key points, draft an email response, and suggest three follow-up questions"
- GPT Image 1.5 generates presentation visuals and social graphics
- Custom GPTs let you build task-specific assistants — meeting agenda generator, email drafter, report summarizer
- Free tier is genuinely generous — same model as paid, just slower at peak times

**Where it falls short:**
- Writing can feel generic without careful prompting
- Free tier introduced ads in February 2026 (US)
- No native meeting integration — can't join your Zoom calls

**Price:** Free / Plus $20/mo

**Get this if:** You want one tool that replaces five. ChatGPT is the first tab you open and the last one you close.

**Skip this if:** You need deep integration with a specific workflow (meetings, presentations, email). Specialized tools beat it in those niches.

## 2. Perplexity — Research at the Speed of Thought

**The pitch:** Perplexity is what happens when a search engine and an AI assistant have a baby. You ask a question. It searches the web, reads the results, and gives you an answer — with citations.

**What it does well:**
- Real-time web search with citations — every claim links to its source
- Pro searches use reasoning models for multi-step research (5 free per day)
- Collections organize research by topic — competitor analysis, market research, product research
- File upload for analyzing PDFs, spreadsheets, and documents

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier limits Pro searches to 5/day — regular searches are unlimited but shallower
- Not a writing tool — it finds and synthesizes information, but won't craft prose
- Less useful offline or for tasks that don't require web search

**Price:** Free (5 Pro searches/day) / Pro $20/mo

**Get this if:** Your work involves any kind of research — market analysis, competitor tracking, fact-checking, or staying current on a topic.

**Skip this if:** You mostly generate original content rather than researching existing information.

---

## 3. Fireflies — The Meeting Note-Taker That Never Tires

**The pitch:** Fireflies joins your Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams call as a silent participant. It records, transcribes, and summarizes everything. You never take meeting notes again.

**What it does well:**
- Joins meetings automatically — invite fred@fireflies.ai and it shows up
- Transcribes in 69 languages with speaker identification
- AI-generated summaries with action items, key topics, and decisions — searchable forever
- Integrates with CRM, Slack, Notion, and project management tools
- Free tier: unlimited transcription

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier limits AI summaries (you get transcript but not smart summaries)
- 800 minutes of storage on free tier for the meeting bot
- The bot appearing in meetings can be awkward — give people a heads up

**Price:** Free (unlimited transcription) / Pro $10/seat/mo

**Get this if:** You sit in more than 3 meetings per week. The time saved on notes alone justifies the signup.

**Skip this if:** Your meetings are sensitive or confidential — having an AI bot recording raises privacy concerns.

---

## 4. Otter — Real-Time Transcription That Feels Like Magic

**The pitch:** Unlike Fireflies (which is a bot), Otter is a live notepad. Open it during a meeting, interview, or lecture, and watch the words appear in real time. You can highlight, comment, and assign action items as they're spoken.

**What it does well:**
- Real-time transcription — words appear as they're spoken, not after the meeting ends
- Highlight and tag key moments live — no rewatching recordings
- Speaker identification — knows who said what
- 300 free minutes per month (resets monthly)
- Mobile app for in-person conversations

**Where it falls short:**
- 300 minutes/month cap — heavy meeting users will hit it
- 30-minute limit per conversation on free tier
- Less automation than Fireflies — more of a live note-taking aid than a set-and-forget bot

**Price:** Free (300 mins/month) / Pro $8/seat/mo

**Get this if:** You conduct interviews, attend lectures, or want to stay engaged in meetings while capturing everything said.

**Skip this if:** You want a set-it-and-forget-it meeting bot. Fireflies is the better fit.

## 5. Gamma — Presentations You Don't Hate Making

**The pitch:** Gamma generates entire presentations, documents, and webpages from a one-line prompt. No template-hunting. No alignment-fighting. Just describe what you need and get a finished deck.

**What it does well:**
- One-click generation — "5-slide pitch deck for an AI scheduling app" produces a real presentation in seconds
- Live embeds: Figma, YouTube, Loom, spreadsheets all render inline
- Built-in analytics — see who viewed your presentation and for how long
- Export to PowerPoint and PDF for offline sharing
- 400 AI credits on free tier (roughly 10 presentations)

**Where it falls short:**
- AI-generated designs sometimes need manual tweaking — it's fast, not perfect
- Free tier presentations are public
- Custom branding requires paid tier

**Price:** Free (400 AI credits) / Plus $8/mo / Pro $15/mo

**Get this if:** You make presentations regularly and hate every minute of it. Gamma turns a 2-hour task into a 2-minute task.

**Skip this if:** You need pixel-perfect, brand-controlled presentations. Hire a designer or use Canva.

## 6. Claude — Deep Work, Deep Analysis

**The pitch:** Claude is ChatGPT's quieter, more analytical sibling. It excels at reading long documents and producing nuanced analysis — the kind of work that requires sustained attention, not quick answers.

**What it does well:**
- 200K context window — upload entire reports, contracts, or books for analysis
- Best long-form coherence of any AI — doesn't forget page 1 by page 5
- Natural, human-sounding analysis — reads like a smart colleague, not a robot
- Free tier accesses the latest model (with daily limits)

**Where it falls short:**
- Daily message cap on free tier (~20-30 messages before downgrading)
- No web search, image generation, or voice mode
- Purely text-based — can't create presentations or join meetings

**Price:** Free / Pro $20/mo

**Get this if:** Your work involves reading and analyzing long, complex documents — legal contracts, research papers, technical reports.

**Skip this if:** You need real-time information or multimedia features. ChatGPT or Perplexity cover those.

## 7. Google Gemini — AI Inside the Apps You Already Use

**The pitch:** Gemini lives inside Gmail, Google Docs, Google Sheets, and Google Drive. If your work life runs on Google Workspace, Gemini is already where you are.

**What it does well:**
- Gmail: summarize threads, draft replies, find old emails with natural language
- Docs: write, rewrite, expand, or summarize — without leaving the document
- Sheets: generate formulas, analyze data, create charts from natural language
- Free tier gives you Gemini 2.5 Pro — a genuinely capable model
- Side panel follows you across apps — consistent AI presence

**Where it falls short:**
- Requires Google Workspace — useless if you're on Microsoft 365
- Creative writing quality lags behind ChatGPT and Claude
- Some features locked behind Google AI Pro ($20/mo)

**Price:** Free / Google AI Pro $20/mo

**Get this if:** You live in Gmail, Docs, and Sheets. Gemini is the path of least resistance.

**Skip this if:** You use Microsoft Office. Microsoft Copilot is your equivalent.

## 8. Microsoft Copilot — Office AI, Minus the Learning Curve

**The pitch:** Copilot is Gemini's counterpart for the Microsoft world. It's built into Windows, Edge, and Office — if you work in Excel, Word, and Teams, this is your AI.

**What it does well:**
- Excel: generate formulas and pivot tables from natural language
- Word: draft, rewrite, and summarize documents inline
- Teams: meeting summaries, action items, and transcript search
- Built into Windows — Copilot key on new PCs, system-wide access
- Free tier gives you full chat access with the latest models

**Where it falls short:**
- Full Office integration requires Microsoft 365 Copilot ($30/user/mo)
- Free tier is mostly chat + basic Office features
- Less polished than ChatGPT for general-purpose writing

**Price:** Free (chat + basic Office) / Pro $20/mo / Microsoft 365 Copilot $30/user/mo

**Get this if:** Your company runs on Microsoft 365. Copilot in Excel alone can save hours per week.

**Skip this if:** You're on Google Workspace. Get Gemini instead.

## 9. Grammarly — Your Second Set of Eyes

**The pitch:** Grammarly doesn't write your content. It catches what you missed — typos, tone-deaf phrasing, clunky sentences. It's the editor that reads everything before you hit send.

**What it does well:**
- Grammar, spelling, punctuation — catches errors you've read past 10 times
- Tone detection — warns you if your email sounds accidentally aggressive
- Works everywhere: browser, desktop, Word, Google Docs, phone
- AI rewrite suggestions (2026 update) — limited on free tier but present

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier lacks clarity, engagement, and delivery suggestions
- English only — no use for multilingual teams
- Premium features ($12/mo) are the ones you actually want

**Price:** Free / Premium $12/mo

**Get this if:** You write anything professional in English. Emails, reports, Slack messages — Grammarly catches the mistakes that make you look careless.

**Skip this if:** You only write in languages other than English.

## 10. Notion AI — Your Second Brain, Now With AI

**The pitch:** Notion AI reads your entire workspace — every doc, database, and project page — and uses that context to answer questions, generate content, and connect ideas you forgot existed.

**What it does well:**
- Q&A across your entire workspace — "what was the Q3 marketing budget?" pulls from the relevant page
- AI writing and editing inside Notion — rewrite, translate, summarize, continue
- Database autofill — generate summaries, tags, and translations for hundreds of rows at once
- Web search integrated — get external context without leaving Notion

**Where it falls short:**
- Notion AI costs $10/month — it's not free, period
- Only works inside Notion — if you leave Notion, you leave the AI behind
- Long-form writing quality doesn't match dedicated tools

**Price:** Notion free / Notion AI $10/mo add-on

**Get this if:** You already live in Notion and want to ask questions about your own knowledge base.

**Skip this if:** You don't use Notion. Don't adopt Notion just for the AI — there are free alternatives.

## FAQ

### Q: Won't AI make me less productive by making me double-check everything?

**A:** This is the right instinct. AI output should be treated as a first draft, not a final answer. But the math is clear: reviewing and editing an AI draft takes 10-30% of the time of creating from scratch. The key is using AI for acceleration, not replacement.

### Q: How do these tools handle privacy and confidential data?

**A:** Varies. ChatGPT and Gemini state that free-tier data may be used for training — don't paste confidential documents. Claude says it doesn't train on user data. Fireflies and Otter store meeting recordings on their servers. Enterprise versions exist for most of these tools with stronger privacy guarantees.

### Q: Can I use these for team productivity, not just personal?

**A:** Most free tiers are designed for individuals. For teams, you'll want paid plans — they add admin controls, shared workspaces, and privacy features. Start with free tiers to test which tools your team actually uses, then upgrade the winners.

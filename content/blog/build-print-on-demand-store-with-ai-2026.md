## Step 1: Generate Product Ideas That Actually Sell

Do not start with "what should I sell?" Start with who you're selling to. Niche beats general every time.

**ChatGPT prompt:**

```
I'm building a print-on-demand merch store for [AUDIENCE].
My brand is about [BRAND POSITIONING].
Give me 10 product ideas that are NOT t-shirts.
For each idea, suggest 3 slogans that would resonate
with this audience.
```

**Example output for "AI/tech audience":**
- Notebook: "Training My Neural Networks" / "This Could Have Been an Email" / "Prompt Engineer In Progress"
- Coffee mug: "Brewing Ideas with AI" / "Coffee → Code → Repeat" / "Natural Language Processor"
- Mouse pad: "Machine Learning: Coffee In, Code Out" / "Debugging My Sanity"

Pick 3-5 products. Start narrow. You can always expand later.

---

## Step 2: Generate the Artwork

**Midjourney prompt template:**

```
An illustration of [DESCRIPTION], black background,
vector style, clean lines, minimal --style raw --ar 1:1
--no text, watermark, signature
```

**Why these settings:**
- `--style raw` — reduces Midjourney's default artsy look
- `--ar 1:1` — square, gives you room to crop
- `--no text` — you'll add text in Printful's editor
- Black background — easy to remove later

Generate 5-10 variations per product. Pick the best one.

---

## Step 3: Post-Process Before Printing

A pixelated print looks unprofessional. Fix it before it reaches the customer.

| Task | Tool | Cost |
|------|------|------|
| Upscale resolution | **Topaz Photo AI** or **Upscale.media** | $99 one-time / Free |
| Remove background | **Adobe Express** (free) | Free |
| Check for AI artifacts | Your eyes | Free |

**The print-quality checklist:**
- [ ] Image resolution at least 300 DPI at print size
- [ ] Background properly removed (no white fringing)
- [ ] No AI artifacts visible when zoomed in
- [ ] Colors look good on both white and dark backgrounds

## Step 4: Set Up Printful (Print-on-Demand)

Printful is the magic that makes this work. You upload a design. Someone orders a product. Printful prints and ships it. You never touch inventory.

1. `printful.com` → Sign up (free)
2. Choose a product: notebooks, mugs, phone cases, tote bags, hoodies
3. Upload your processed artwork
4. Use Printful's built-in editor to add text, adjust placement, and pick fonts
5. Generate mockup photos — these become your product images on Shopify
6. Set your retail price (cost + your margin)

**Pricing tip:** Printful shows you the base cost. Add $5-15 margin per product. A mug that costs $8 to print sells for $18-24.

---

## Step 5: Build the Shopify Store

1. `shopify.com` → Start Free Trial ($1 for 3 months)
2. Install the **Printful app** from the Shopify App Store (one click)
3. Sync your Printful products to Shopify (one click)
4. Use **Shopify's built-in AI** to write product descriptions:
   - Click "Generate Text" on the product page
   - Select tone (Playful / Professional)
   - Edit the AI output to match your brand voice
5. Customize the store theme: pick a clean template, add your logo, set colors

---

## Step 6: Launch and Promote

- Add your store link to your social media bios
- Create one "launch" post per platform
- Share the story: "I built this store in an hour with AI"
- Consider a limited-time discount to drive first sales

**The first sale is the hardest.** After that, you have proof the model works.

## FAQ

### Q: What if nobody buys?

**A:** Your only cost is $1 (Shopify trial) and your time. Printful charges nothing until an order comes in. There is no inventory risk. If it doesn't work, you lost an hour and $1.

### Q: What products should I start with?

**A:** Notebooks, mugs, and tote bags. They're simple. No sizing issues. No color variations. Printful handles them reliably. Graduating to apparel after you've validated demand.
